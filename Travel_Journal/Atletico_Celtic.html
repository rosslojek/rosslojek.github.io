Champions League Group Stage: Atletico Madrid vs. Glasgow Celtic

Date: Tuesday 11/7/2023

Location: Madrid

Yesterday on a walking tour of Madrid I met a German named Johannes, and we got along well. Not only was he a soccer fan, but we both had tickets to Atletico Madrid vs. Glasgow Celtic, and our seats were only two sections apart. What a coincidence!

We agreed to meet at the main plaza and go to the game together. As it turned out, Celtic supporters had the same idea and we ended up smack in the middle of the visiting fans as they traveled from the center of Madrid to the Civitas Metropolitan.

Over the last two days or so there had been a noticeable amount of Scots in town. By noticeable, I mean anywhere I looked, anywhere in the city, I would spot a group of three bald guys with hands on hips and a full-zip sweatshirt tied around waist exposing the unmistakable green and white stripes. They fit in like clowns at a funeral amongst the Spaniards.

The powers at be, both the Scottish supporters clubs and the Spanish authorities, orchestrated the entire trip. Surrounded by police, we were herded into a subway station, leaving behind the obvious debris of any drunken mob. The station, packed shoulder to shoulder from the entrance stairway all the way to the platform, was absorbed by waves of pandemonium as they broke out in the songs of their homeland. One person would start, and the station would erupt, "We're Glasgow Celtic, We're the best team in Scotland," accompanied by the banging of trash barrels, ceiling panels, and the station walls.

Riot police finally got us to the platform, where an entire train had been reserved for the traveling fans. My estimate is 11 cars for fans and at least one car reserved for just riot police.

When we made it to the stadium, Johannes and I, being grouped in with the away supporters, were corralled into the away fan entrance, despite our actual tickets being on the opposite side of the stadium. There was no negotiating with the riot police, though, and when we saw a Celtic fan get roughed up we decided not to test their patience. Inside the next checkpoint, we found a more reasonable security guard, and we eventually made it to our section.

The Celtic section was probably 6,000-8,000 people, but there were other Scots everywhere in the stadium; maybe 10,000-12,000 total, or 15% of the total crowd.

Celtic came in as the underdog, and it was understood that they would need a near-perfect performance to leave Madrid with points. In an unfortunate turn of events, this plan fell apart when Griezmann put Atletico 1-0 up on a half-chance at 6 minutes. Bad start for Celtic. 20 minutes in and things went from bad to worse, as Celtic went down to 10 on a red card that should never have been called, especially because VAR overruled the call on the field. At this point, the game was basically finished, and the first half ended 2-0 to Madrid. If the first half was bad, the second half was noncompetitive.

What was worse than Celtic conceding their 5th and 6th goals was that it was in front of an increasingly empty stadium as, after traveling all that distance to support their team, the Scottish fans had seen enough, and most of them had left the stadium by that point.

FT Atletico 6 - 0 Celtic

Griezmann played really well, scoring two goals and creating many more chances as Atletico’s most creative player.

On the subway back there was one lonely Scot in our car who continued to sing his heart out, insisting between verses that they “were good in Scotland.” He couldn’t even see straight.
