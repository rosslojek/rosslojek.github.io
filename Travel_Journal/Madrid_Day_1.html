Day 1 in Madrid, Flea Market

Date: Sunday 11/5/2023

Location: Madrid

Arrived by train [from Sevilla] late last night in Madrid.

My Hostel, 2060 Newton, will be hard to beat for the rest of my trip. Clean, comfortable, private, and social; perfect combination. They provide sleeping ‘modules;’ first time I have seen this, but I am a fan.

I left my hostel for the first time to check out the city. I walked one block in a direction of my choice, and next thing I knew the streets were full of people in a street sale/flea market. For the next few hours I would wander through the rows full of people buying and selling just about anything you can think of. It changed from block to block: some streets were selling clothes, others books, others plants, and others that seemed to be selling anything for the right price. On one corner there were three poets set up, and for a few bucks they would cut a poem on an old typewriter. They looked so much like poets that I don’t even think it was a costume.

The market seemed to go on forever, and it was the perfect day of unplanned exploration in a new city. I ended up buying Cole a 2012 Spain Euro jersey with ‘Cruzcampo’ as the name on the back.

Sundays are a great day for exploring these cities. Saturdays are often about the night, but Sundays are about the daytime. It felt like everyone was out, enjoying the day and enjoying their city.
