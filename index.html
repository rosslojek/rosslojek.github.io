About Me

I am a learner. I am passionate about using data to improve our understanding of problems and solutions, particularly as it relates to equity and sustainability. As a natural communicator, I am well suited to collaboration and presentation of findings. I have experience in data analysis, data visualization, econometrics, and business intelligence in R, Tableau, SQL, and Python, as well as programming experience in Python, Java, and C++.

My research interests are on the intersection of labor markets and technology. How can we best leverage technology to improve social welfare? My highlighted work below, titled, “The Logistic Marginal Farebox,” is an example of this research as it relates to the gig economy.

In my free time, I spend time with friends and family, when I am not either playing or watching soccer. When asked, I tell people my favorite team is Crystal Palace, but I really just enjoy watching the passion, creativity, and cultural diversity of the game.
